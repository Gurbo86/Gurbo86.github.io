ABOUT RUST CONSOLE

Rust Console Edition brings the acclaimed PC survival sandbox to Xbox & PlayStation — now with full cross-play. Up to 100 players scavenge, build and raid across procedurally generated worlds that wipe every month, so every season feels new. Since launch, the franchise has sold 12 million+ copies worldwide and still draws 100 000+ concurrent survivors daily, securing its place among the most-played console titles.

FEATURES

  • Persistent open-world PvP survival with monthly wipes
  • Deep crafting & base-building system
  • Dynamic raiding & siege combat with explosives
  • Multiple Modular systems for electricity, fluids & industrial automation
  • Rideable horses, boats, submarines & helicopters
  • Controller-first UI with accessibility options
  • Regular free content updates and live events

Project Description

As the sole Game Designer involved in the development of Rust Console during this period, I often had to take on Technical and UI/UX tasks. I pitched and prototyped UI proposals, controller adaptations, and systems for community servers. Between my everyday tasks, I investigated the mechanics to be introduced into the game, made adaptation and improvement proposals tailored for the console, produced end-to-end documentation, and provided support to the team during the implementation process. On release, I used to check community receptions, looking for feedback to incorporate in the QoL releases.

Project Contributions

  • Leaded the all the design tasks of the project.
  • Delivered all design tasks in a tight 3 month time pipeline.
  • Clear cross department comunication, relying mostly on production and QA.
  • Spearheaded the integration old and future documentation to a Notion Wiki.
  • Investigated the code and documented preexisting mechanics, in math notation with Katex, for future designers.
  • Tested and modified through Unity Engine and C# code, several new mechanics.
  • Provided full design proposals for comunity servers tools, from ideas to UI/UX interfaces with full support for console.
  • In the last months I mentored and leaded placements designers.

This way players could trade resources, weapons, clothing, and other items in a new way.

Horsepocalypse

Horsepocalypse was the first thoroughly hands-on Development in this project. In this, I introduced the ridable horses, which pushed me to find a way to provide control to the players of this new vehicle. This particular change facilitated the standardization of vehicle controllers, thereby simplifying their understanding. Additionally, I re-designed the UI of the horse's inventory and its item mechanics. Horse acquisition was designed from scratch, taking into account the interactions involved. We innovated from PCs by incorporating two bars that provided the state of the horses (PC introduced later down the road). We improved players' mission menus, which opened the door for a future tool to enable community servers to create their missions.

Here I leave a link to the DEV BLOG #20. , which were used to inform the players.

Below I add some of the things I worked on for this update:

  • Full Design of horses' interactions, controls, and UI's.
  • Design and established the standard for vehicle controls.
  • Documentation of how horses provided of a new resource... dung💩.
  • Documentation and balancing horses' inventory items.
  • Improvement of mission UI.

Cultivator

With Cultivator, I was given my first opportunity to work in UI and game communication with the player. Crops were a feature that players didn't exploit much on PC, so I tried to make the UI more communicative to help players understand the consequences of their actions. We introduced also the Mixing Table enable players to craft boosting teas. We introduced the fluid system that allows players to water their crops.

Here I leave a link to the DEV BLOG #17. , which were used to inform the players.

Below I add some of the things I worked on for this update:

  • Full UI redesign of the crops UI. Now, players have better tools to predict a plant's future genes.
  • Introduction and design improvement of the fluid system. We added additional UI to explain fluid pressure.
  • Adapted controls for the mixing table, making it possible to use properly on the console.
